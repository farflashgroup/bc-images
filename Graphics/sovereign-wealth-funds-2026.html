TL;DR

Sovereign wealth funds are generally used to park a country's excess wealth, usually from natural resources or trade surpluses, into global stocks, bonds, real estate, and infrastructure. The goal is to preserve and grow that wealth for future generations, stabilize government revenue during downturns, and reduce dependence on a single resource.

With our vast oil and critical mineral wealth, Canada has been a natural candidate. A national sovereign wealth fund could do federally what Alberta's Heritage Fund does provincially: convert resource royalties into long-term financial assets.

The recently announced Canada Strong Fund would be debt-funded, unlike most SWF's which use surplus-funding or hybrid models.
