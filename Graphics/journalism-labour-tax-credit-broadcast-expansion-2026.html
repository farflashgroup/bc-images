Should Ottawa help pay TV news salaries?

35%

Paid back
to the employer

There is already a program where the federal government sends money back to some news employers for reporter payroll. Eligible: mainly print and qualifying digital newsrooms - not TV or radio news. The cut is thirty-five cents on each dollar of salary they can claim - more than a third - under the rules. Stations care because it lowers their staffing bill. What Ottawa is weighing now: letting TV and radio news qualify for it too.

Before

TV and radio were left out. Only print-style and online shops could ask for the subsidy.

Changing

Ottawa wants input on adding TV and radio newsrooms to the same deal.

Means

Bell, Corus, Rogers-scale newsrooms could tap the same subsidy for crews that cover Ottawa - money from the same public chequebook that funds ministers’ offices.

Conflict

Viewers may ask: how neutral is the news when the people you cover help pay the crew?
