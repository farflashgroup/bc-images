What Bill C-22 would change

Where C-22 changes the path

C-22 makes private metadata easier to preserve and obtain.

Tech companies

≤1y

New duty

Retention

  • More metadata about accounts and communications could be kept for later access.
  • Information that might disappear today could remain available for up to one year.

New duty

Capability

  • Providers may need to build systems that make data easier to preserve and extract.
  • Those duties could weaken privacy protections by design.

Modified tool

Access orders

  • Police and CSIS get clearer paths to account, device, and transmission data.
  • Users may never know their provider was asked to preserve or hand over records.

Existing check

Oversight

  • Most compulsory access still needs judicial authorization.
  • The privacy concern is a larger pool of data becoming easier to demand.

Still excluded on paper Message content, browsing history, and social feeds are carved out of the retention rules, but the technical duties could still weaken privacy.

Source: Parliament of Canada, Bill C-22 first reading (Mar. 12, 2026).
